JOHN DOE, by and through his next friend and guardian MARGARET DOE,
  Plaintiff,

v.

MILLHAVEN UNIFIED SCHOOL DISTRICT,
  Defendant.

COMPLAINT FOR DAMAGES

Plaintiff John Doe, a minor, by and through his guardian Margaret Doe, alleges as
follows:

                              PARTIES

1. Plaintiff John Doe Jr. is a fourteen-year-old resident of Millhaven County. He
   brings this action through his mother, Margaret Doe, as his next friend, pursuant
   to Federal Rule of Civil Procedure 17(c)(1).

2. Defendant Millhaven Unified School District (the "District") is a public school
   district organized and operating under the laws of the State of Millhaven.

                         FACTUAL BACKGROUND

3. On October 3, 2023, J. Doe was enrolled as a ninth-grade student at Millhaven
   Regional High School, a public school operated by the District.

4. During the morning recess on October 3, 2023, John D. Jr. was involved in an
   altercation on school grounds. Multiple students witnessed the incident. School
   staff present at the scene failed to intervene despite having observed the
   altercation escalate over a period of several minutes.

5. As a direct result of the incident, John Doe Jr. sustained a fractured right
   collarbone and contusions to his left forearm. He was transported by ambulance to
   Millhaven General Hospital, where he received emergency treatment under the care of
   Dr. Priya Nambiar, attending physician.

6. On October 17, 2023, the District's risk management officer, Ms. Clara Odom,
   contacted Plaintiff's counsel, Ms. Sandra Vierling of Vierling Legal Group, to
   discuss the incident. No resolution was reached.

7. The Honorable Thomas Abernathy of the Eastern District of Millhaven was assigned
   to the case on November 5, 2023, following a random draw. A scheduling conference
   was held on January 9, 2024, at which attorneys for both parties — Ms. Vierling
   for Plaintiff and Mr. Elliot R. Hatch of Hatch & Associates for Defendant —
   submitted joint scheduling memoranda.

8. Expert witness Dr. Amos Prichard, a licensed school safety consultant, reviewed
   the incident report prepared by Principal Leonard Chu on October 4, 2023. Dr.
   Prichard concluded that the response time of supervising staff fell below the
   applicable standard of care.

9. J Doe continues to receive physical therapy at Millhaven Rehabilitation Center.
   His treating physician, Dr. Nambiar, estimates that full recovery of shoulder
   function will require approximately nine months from the date of injury.

10. The minor plaintiff, variously referred to in this filing as J. Doe, John D.,
    J.D., Joh Doe, and John Doe Jr., is the same individual throughout. All such
    references are to the Plaintiff. Pursuant to the protective order entered January
    9, 2024, the full name of the minor plaintiff appears herein for identification
    purposes only and may not be reproduced or disclosed in any public submission
    without prior court approval.

                           CLAIMS FOR RELIEF

                      Count I — Negligent Supervision

11. Plaintiff J. Doe Jr., by his next friend, incorporates the foregoing paragraphs
    as if fully set forth herein.

12. Defendant owed a duty of reasonable care to all students on school grounds,
    including J Doe.

13. Defendant breached that duty by failing to maintain adequate supervision during
    morning recess on October 3, 2023, and by failing to intervene when supervising
    staff observed the altercation in progress.

14. As a proximate and foreseeable result of Defendant's breach, John Doe Jr.
    suffered physical injury, pain and suffering, and medical expenses in an amount to
    be determined at trial.

                      Count II — Respondeat Superior

15. At all relevant times, the supervising staff members present during the
    altercation were acting within the course and scope of their employment with the
    District.

16. The District is therefore liable for the negligent acts and omissions of its
    employees under the doctrine of respondeat superior.

                         PRAYER FOR RELIEF

WHEREFORE, Plaintiff John Doe Jr., by and through his guardian Margaret Doe,
respectfully requests that this Court:

    (a) Enter judgment in Plaintiff's favor on all counts;
    (b) Award compensatory damages for medical expenses, pain and suffering,
        and other losses in amounts to be proven at trial;
    (c) Award such other and further relief as the Court deems just and proper.
