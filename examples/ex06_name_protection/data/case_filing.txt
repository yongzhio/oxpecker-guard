COMPLAINT FOR DAMAGES

                              PARTIES

1. Plaintiff John Doe Jr. is a fourteen-year-old resident of Millhaven County. He
   brings this action through his guardian Margaret Doe pursuant to FRCP 17(c)(1).

2. Defendant Millhaven Unified School District operates Millhaven Regional High School
   where the incident occurred.

                         FACTUAL BACKGROUND

3. On October 3, 2023, J. Doe was enrolled as a ninth-grade student at Millhaven
   Regional High School.

4. During morning recess, John D. Jr. was involved in an altercation on school
   grounds. Staff present failed to intervene despite observing the escalation.

5. John Doe Jr. sustained a fractured right collarbone. He was transported to
   Millhaven General Hospital and treated by Dr. Priya Nambiar.

6. Defense counsel Mr. Elliot R. Hatch and Plaintiff's counsel Ms. Sandra Vierling
   attended a scheduling conference before the Honorable Thomas Abernathy on January
   9, 2024. Expert Dr. Amos Prichard concluded that staff response fell below the
   applicable standard of care.

7. J Doe continues physical therapy. Dr. Nambiar estimates nine months to full
   recovery. All references to J. Doe, John D., J.D., and John Doe Jr. in this
   filing refer to the same minor plaintiff.

                           CLAIMS FOR RELIEF

8. Plaintiff John Doe Jr. alleges that Defendant breached its duty of care by failing
   to supervise students adequately during morning recess on October 3, 2023.

9. As a proximate result of Defendant's breach, J. Doe Jr. suffered physical injury,
   pain and suffering, and medical expenses to be determined at trial.

WHEREFORE, Plaintiff respectfully requests judgment in his favor on all counts.
